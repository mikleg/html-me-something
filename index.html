My notes

All my adventures took place in Russia, in the Ural Mountains.

Unlike the United States, in Russia there is no such developed tourism. There are no paved routes, equipped areas, places for camping and even toilets! All tourists rely only on themselves and carry everything they need with them in their backpack, even water. There is almost impossible to buy weapons or special means for protection from wild animals in Russia. And although meetings with the bears are not so frequent, in such cases tourists rely only on their cunning and knowledge.

But the bear attack, thirst and the chance to get lost are not the most dangerous things in the hikes. The Ural tourists are most afraid of the bite of a small insect - a tick bite, because about 12% of ticks in the Urals are infected with Tick-borne encephalitis A significant part of infected ticks has a Siberian variety of the virus, the mortality of which is about 80%.

Therefore, you can see in the photos many of my friends have such ridiculous shoes and high socks. Those who did not wear such equipment were forced to treat themselves with a special spray every 2 hours. Despite of all the obstacles, hiking in Russia is quite popular with one exception: almost all hiking trips have a duration of several days. Primarily it happens because of the long way to start by the car and general underdevelopment of the infrastructure (usually 4-6 hours per one way). One-day hikes are usually done only to pick up mushrooms and berries, for fishing or hunting.

Urenga. July 2002

Urenga is the longest mountains chain in the South Ural mountains.It was my first hiking trip there. All photos were taken by our first digital camera - 1.3 Mpx Olympus! The most remembering event was during our second night: there was a bear walking around our tent. We knew about it only after our wake-up!

Zuratkul. June 2004

Zuratkul is a state park in South Ural mountains. It is well known because it include the highest lake in Ural mountains. Unlike our's usual routes, there was some cabines on territory. Therefore we went to a top without our's backpacks. All photos were taken by our first digital camera - 1.3 Mpx Olympus! The most remembering event was snow in June!
